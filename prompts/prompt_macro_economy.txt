Create a global economic outlook for the next month in #file:global_economic_outlook.md.

Goal:
- Explain what is happening in the world right now that affects stock trading.
- Explain how those conditions are likely to change over the next month.
- Reassess the macro environment with fresh eyes each day rather than assuming yesterday's dominant narrative is still correct.
- Clearly separate what is still driving markets from what is new today or newly important.

Focus areas:
- Geopolitics that affect markets, especially energy supply, trade routes, and risk sentiment
- Central bank policy and rate expectations
- Inflation, bond yields, and growth expectations
- Regional differences across the U.S., Europe, China, and Japan
- Which sectors or styles are likely to outperform or underperform in the next month

Output requirements:
- Write for an investor or trader, not for an academic audience.
- Keep the analysis practical and directly tied to stock-market implications.
- Include a clear base case for the next month.
- Include bull, base, and bear scenarios if the macro picture is highly uncertain.
- End with a short bottom-line summary.
- Explicitly identify:
	- which prior market drivers are still in force,
	- what is genuinely new today,
	- whether the market narrative has shifted, weakened, or broadened,
	- and which risks the market may still be underpricing.
- Do not anchor on the previous day's thesis without re-validating it against today's developments and market moves.
- If yesterday's dominant driver is no longer the main driver, say so clearly and replace it with the updated hierarchy of risks.

Suggested structure:
- Bottom line
- What is still driving markets
- What is new today
- What is happening right now
- What will likely change over the next month
- What traders should watch most closely
- Sector and regional implications
- Scenarios
- Summary

Style guidance:
- Be concise, clear, and market-focused.
- Avoid generic macro commentary that does not affect stocks.
- Prioritize the biggest drivers first.
- Treat this as a fresh daily diagnosis, not a lightly edited carry-forward from the prior note.
- Be explicit about changes in tone, direction, and ranking of risks.
