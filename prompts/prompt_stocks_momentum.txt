Identify a shortlist of stocks that may be entering the early stage of a momentum move before the broad market fully notices.

Goal:
- Find stocks early enough for a swing trade, before the obvious breakout or full retail chase.
- Focus on public, legal, observable signals only.
- Do not rely on or suggest material nonpublic information, leaked insider information, or anything illegal.
- Prefer setups where momentum appears to be building because of a real catalyst, quiet accumulation, or improving market attention.

Core idea:
- The objective is not to predict random pumps.
- The objective is to find stocks where a credible catalyst and early tape behavior suggest an asymmetric near-term attention shift.

What to look for:

1. Catalyst proximity
- Look for a real event within roughly the next 2 to 8 weeks.
- Examples:
	- earnings,
	- guidance reset,
	- investor day,
	- product launch,
	- FDA or trial milestone,
	- court ruling,
	- contract award,
	- merger or spin-off event,
	- index inclusion,
	- regulatory decision,
	- commodity or policy event likely to reprice a sector.
- Prefer catalysts that are specific and dateable, not vague company promises.

2. Early accumulation and volume behavior
- Look for rising volume before a major price breakout.
- Prefer names where:
	- 5-day average volume is clearly above 20-day average volume,
	- volume is expanding on up days,
	- the stock repeatedly closes in the upper part of its daily range,
	- accumulation appears over several sessions rather than one isolated spike.

3. Relative strength before the crowd arrives
- Prefer stocks that are already outperforming their sector or small-cap peers quietly.
- Useful signs:
	- the stock stops falling even when its group is weak,
	- it holds key moving averages better than peers,
	- pullbacks are shallow and bought quickly,
	- price compresses tightly after an initial move instead of fully retracing.

4. Options and positioning clues
- If options data is available, look for unusual call activity, rising open interest, or implied volatility building around a plausible catalyst.
- Treat this as supporting evidence only, not as a standalone reason.

5. Float and tradability
- Prefer stocks with enough liquidity to trade cleanly but not such a large float that attention cannot move them.
- Favor names with:
	- sufficient daily dollar volume,
	- no obvious near-term dilution,
	- no imminent financing overhang,
	- no extreme illiquidity,
	- a float small enough that new attention can matter.

6. Fundamental and narrative support
- Prefer stocks where the story is easy for the market to understand in one sentence.
- The setup is stronger if at least one of these is true:
	- earnings revisions are improving,
	- margins or cash flow are inflecting,
	- a prior bearish thesis is weakening,
	- short interest is elevated while fundamentals are improving,
	- the company is entering a better industry tape or macro regime.

Exclude:
- Obvious promotion-driven names with no real catalyst
- Severe dilution risk, going-concern risk, or serial capital raises
- Reverse-split quality situations unless there is an unusually strong and specific catalyst
- Single-day volume anomalies without follow-through
- Story stocks whose only support comes from social-media hype
- Situations where the “edge” appears to depend on leaked insider information or other nonpublic data

Output requirements:
- Record findings in #file:momentum_stocks.md in a practical swing-trading format.
- Use a markdown table.
- Return only names that genuinely look early, not already fully extended momentum leaders.
- If few names qualify, return fewer names rather than forcing marginal setups.
- If no names qualify cleanly, say so and provide the closest watchlist names.

Include these columns:
- Ticker
- Company
- Market Cap
- Catalyst Window
- Early Momentum Signal
- Volume / Relative Strength Clue
- Why The Market May Notice Soon
- Main Reason It Is Still Early
- Key Risk

Suggested method:
- Start with a liquid small-cap and mid-cap universe.
- Narrow to names with a dated upcoming catalyst or a clear sector-specific trigger.
- Check for abnormal but not fully euphoric volume behavior.
- Check whether the stock is outperforming quietly before a full breakout.
- Prefer setups with a clean narrative and no obvious capital-structure trap.
- Separate true candidates from “already pumped” names.

Decision rule:
- A strong candidate should usually have at least:
	- one credible near-term catalyst,
	- one clear early accumulation signal,
	- one sign of relative strength,
	- and no major balance-sheet or dilution red flags.

Style guidance:
- Be concise, skeptical, and practical.
- Focus on evidence that can be seen in public market behavior.
- Avoid breathless language like “moon,” “explosive,” or “guaranteed breakout.”
- The goal is early but tradable momentum, not promotional speculation.
