## Notes

- DLNG and GASS are the two cleanest March 25 fits because both keep direct exposure to route disruption and energy friction without leaning entirely on a fresh crude spike.
- I kept direct E&P exposure out of the core list because the macro note now points to a relief-rally regime with unresolved supply risk, not a confirmed straight-line oil breakout.
- ASC stays tradable, but with the asset discount mostly gone it is now more of a freight-momentum name than a hard-value name.
- I excluded a wider set of banks, insurers, retailers, and more aggressive cyclicals because either the tangible-book cushion was not real enough, the cash-flow test was too weak, or the macro sensitivity was too high for the stated objective.

## Method

- Market cap, profitability, and trading data were screened for profitable names below USD 750M, then filtered through the March 25 macro view.
- Tangible book discount used the latest reported tangible book value or the closest practical asset-value equivalent divided by current shares outstanding.
- DCF discount used a conservative zero-growth valuation on normalized free cash flow with a 12% discount rate and cash minus total debt adjustment, while giving extra weight to recent cash-flow durability rather than peak-cycle years.
- For financials and insurers, cash-flow-based DCF can be misleading, so I did not force those names into the table unless they were compelling on both resilience and valuation.
