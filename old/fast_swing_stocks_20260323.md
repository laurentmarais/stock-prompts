## Notes

- Widening the cap to USD 750M did not materially improve the strict shortlist; it mainly brought ASC back into view as a broader-cap near-pass.
- DLNG and GASS remain the strongest names because they still combine profitability, balance-sheet support, asset backing, and a clear macro tailwind.
- AMPY remains the best conditional oil re-spike idea, but it still belongs outside the core list because the conservative DCF does not validate it.
- I excluded several otherwise cheap names because they looked too exposed to governance risk, credit stress, weak liquidity, or unstable cash-flow quality for the stated objective.

## Method

- Market cap, profitability, and trading data were screened first for sub-$600M profitable names and then rechecked through a widened sub-$750M pass.
- Tangible book discount used the latest reported tangible book value or closest practical asset-value equivalent divided by current shares outstanding.
- DCF discount used a conservative zero-growth valuation on normalized cash flow with a 12% discount rate and net cash or debt adjustment.
- For financials and insurers, cash-flow-based DCF can be misleading, so I did not force those names into the table unless they were compelling on both resilience and valuation.
