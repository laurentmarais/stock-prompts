# Fast Swing Stocks

Date: March 26, 2026

## Scope

This screen uses the March 26 macro outlook as the top-down filter.

The current backdrop still favors names that can hold up if:

- oil stays elevated,
- shipping risk remains unresolved or broadens,
- yields remain firm,
- and broad equities stay volatile rather than trending cleanly higher.

That pushes the screen toward profitable, asset-backed, smaller-cap names in areas that can benefit from supply disruption or at least stay relatively insulated from the main macro risks. It pushes the screen away from consumer-sensitive, refinancing-sensitive, and purely tactical low-quality bounces.

The cleanest current fits are still concentrated in shipping-linked energy exposure rather than in broad cyclicals. That is not because shipping is hardcoded into the method, but because it is where the March 26 macro note still points after reassessing the regime with fresh eyes.

## Notes

- DLNG and GASS remain the cleanest March 26 fits because they combine profitability, hard-asset backing, valuation support, and a macro backdrop that still favors shipping-linked energy friction.
- The current macro note is more cautious than the prior relief-rally framing, which makes balance-sheet quality and downside cushion more important than chasing higher-beta tactical rebounds.
- I kept direct upstream energy exposure out of the core list because the current regime still supports oil-linked names, but it is now more contested and headline-driven than a clean straight-line crude breakout.
- I did not force in defense-adjacent or industrial names without clear dual support from both valuation and business quality, even if the macro narrative is favorable.

## Method

- Used the March 26 macro outlook as the top-down filter rather than assuming any fixed regime in advance.
- Preferred sectors and business models that appear more resilient to the active risks in the macro note: elevated oil, unresolved shipping stress, tighter financial conditions, and regional fragility outside the U.S.
- Required profitability, acceptable balance-sheet quality, tangible asset support or a close equivalent, and conservative DCF support.
- Returned fewer names rather than weakening the screen with fragile or low-quality tactical setups.
